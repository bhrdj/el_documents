### From "Motor, brakes and pilot controls - The overall metaphor.docx"

#### 1. **Scenario: Amani vs. Baraka** (Added to Section 4.1)
**Location**: Beginning of "4.1 Child Development in General"
- Compares 8-month-old Amani (minimal interaction) vs Baraka (engaged caregiving)
- Shows how interaction drives holistic development
- Establishes that EL caregivers are "child developers" not just babysitters
- **Key Message**: "Children who are only tended to develop slowly; Children who are interacted with develop holistically"

#### 2. **Scenario: Chege vs. Duru** (Added to Section 4.5)
**Location**: Beginning of "4.5 Preserving the Privilege of Learning"
- Compares 18-month-old Chege (negative reading environment) vs Duru (reading as play)
- Perfect lead-in to the privilege philosophy
- Reinforces that books should be "candy" not chores
- **Key Message**: "Which child is likely to love reading? Which child is likely to love Math?"

#### 3. **Enhanced Motor/Brakes/Pilot Metaphor** (Section 4.2.1)
**Location**: "4.2.1 Motor, Brakes, and Pilot Controls - The Overall Metaphor"
- Clarified the three components with better formatting
- **Motor**: EL techniques that utilize child's energy, curiosity, emotions
- **Safety Brakes**: Gentle discipline when needed (calmly, without shame)
- **Pilot Controls**: Self-motivation and independence for older children
- **Gari and Ndege Application**:
  - Gari class: focus on motor and brakes (cause/effect developing)
  - Ndege class: ready for pilot controls (self-motivation)

#### 4. **The Safety Brake Guidelines** (Added to Section 4.3.0)
**Location**: New subsection "4.3.0 The Safety Brake: When to Discipline"
- Clear car brake metaphor for when to apply discipline
- **Use Safety Brake when**:
  - Child is overexcited or aggressive
  - Unsafe behavior is shown
  - Child is upset and needs calm
- **DO NOT use for**:
  - Curiosity
  - Mistakes
  - Normal fussiness
- **Key Message**: "Safety Brake includes gently positive rewards and gently negative consequences. These increase discipline but decrease creativity, so use them thoughtfully and only when necessary."
